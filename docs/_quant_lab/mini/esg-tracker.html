What it does

Visualizes ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) scores across companies and sectors using radar charts, grouped bar charts, heatmaps, and ranked bar charts. Helps investors evaluate companies beyond pure financials.

The math

Each company gets three pillar scores (E, S, G) on a 0-100 scale, plus a total composite. Radar charts expose the shape of a profile -- a company might score high overall but have a single weak pillar. Sector averages reveal structural biases (energy firms score low on E by nature).

When to use it

Use this for ESG screening and comparison. Scores vary dramatically across providers (MSCI, Sustainalytics, S&P often disagree). The heatmap is the fastest way to scan many companies at once -- red cells jump out as areas needing attention.

Core function

import pandas as pd
import plotly.graph_objects as go


esg_data = pd.DataFrame([
    {"company": "AAPL",  "sector": "Technology",  "E": 78, "S": 72, "G": 85, "total": 78},
    {"company": "MSFT",  "sector": "Technology",  "E": 82, "S": 80, "G": 88, "total": 83},
    {"company": "XOM",   "sector": "Energy",      "E": 40, "S": 55, "G": 70, "total": 55},
    {"company": "JNJ",   "sector": "Healthcare",  "E": 74, "S": 82, "G": 78, "total": 78},
    {"company": "NEE",   "sector": "Utilities",   "E": 88, "S": 70, "G": 76, "total": 78},
])


def esg_radar(companies: list[str], data: pd.DataFrame) -> go.Figure:
    """Build a radar chart comparing ESG pillars across companies."""
    categories = ["Environmental", "Social", "Governance"]
    fig = go.Figure()
    for company in companies:
        row = data[data["company"] == company].iloc[0]
        values = [row["E"], row["S"], row["G"]]
        fig.add_trace(go.Scatterpolar(
            r=values + [values[0]],
            theta=categories + [categories[0]],
            name=company, fill="toself", opacity=0.6,
        ))
    fig.update_layout(polar=dict(radialaxis=dict(range=[0, 100])))
    return fig

Quick usage

fig = esg_radar(["AAPL", "MSFT", "XOM"], esg_data)
fig.show()
